SECTION 1: Business and Data Visualisation Context¶

Introduction¶

YourFirm.de is one of Germany's online leading job board that focuses in small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs), known in German as the Mittelstand. Yourfirm is a public company which operates in the delivery services through an online platform offering a job opportunities for professionals, managers, and employers in the mid-sized companies.

This notebook examines a dataset gathered from the YourFirm website in March 2026. Each data holds information about the job title, hiring company, geographic location (city, state, country, and GPS coordinates), job categories, and posting dates.

Our aim is to make an explanatory data analysis pipeline that analyzes data to generate meaningful insights. Accordingly, we focus on answering questions such as:

  1. Where are jobs concentrated geographically?
  2. Which cities have the most listings, and are there underserved regions?
  3. Which companies are hiring?
  4. Which companies maintain the highest market share on the platform, and How concentrated is the market?
  5. Which job categories are in demand?
  6. How is the market globally recognized? What proportion of job titles are presented in English and German?
  7. When do recruiters post?

The audience for this analysis is the management team at YourFirm.de, who can use these insights to guide marketing strategy, identify growth opportunities, and improve the platform experience for both employers and job seekers.

What do we want our audience to know or do?¶

We want the leadership team to understand the structure and trends hidden in their own platform data. Accordingly, we want the leadership team to do the following things:

  • Point out which geographic regions are well-served and which are underprivileged, so they can target expansion efforts.
  • Recognize which companies and industries stand over their platform,and to determine client concentration risk.
  • Analyze hiring trends such as the enlarging usage of English language in job titles.
  • Leverage these analytical findings to optimize data-driven decisions across marketing channels, employer partnerships, and platform utilities.

What kind of data do we have?¶

The Yourfirm.de dataset contains job listings posted in March 2026. Each record has 19 columns including:

  • Job information: title, description (position_addition), categories
  • Company information: company name, contact details
  • Location: region/city, state, country, postal code, GPS coordinates
  • Timing: posting start date, scrape timestamp

SECTION 3: Data Preprocessing¶

Now that we understand our dataset having 47,750 job posting rows, 19 columns, with significant missing values in several fields, duplicate records, and non-informative coloumns. Data cleaning is required for the dataset before visualization. The assessment brief asks

  1. Remove Duplicate - We can remove duplicates using source ID that are not useful.
  2. Drop non-informative columns — We can remove columns that are 95–100% empty.
  3. Eliminate low value columns — We can remove categories and subcategories, contact_phone, application_url, contact_email,scraped_at, source_url which holds the data we dont need for our analysis.
  4. Address missing values — We fill geographic NaNs with 'Unknown'
  5. Convert date columns — We render string dates into datetime objects

From our analysis which is based on Yourfirm.de dataset, we have tried to understand how this company plays a role in job opportunities for job seekers holding different information related to different job categories. We have explained about the seven meaningful insights and answered based on the visualization context. This analysis examined over 47,750 job postings from yourfirm dataset using Germany and Austria as an example.

Regarding job posting activities, jobs are posted more on weekdays suggesting structured recruitment styles. We have also found that job listings in Germany are heavily concentrated in the Western and Southern German regions(Bayern, NRW, Baden-Wurttemberg), while the Eastern part remains unprivileged in a high growth opportunity. The analysis indicates job markets related to manufacturing are written in German, which indicates the market is local and industry based though some jobs are written in English. However, the dataset shows certain limitations which include the abscence of salary information, numeric codes without labels, and restricted time frame of only one month which limits the depth of insights.

To build up the platform, we have made some recommendations such as expanding the economic outcomes in Eastern Germany which brings growth opportunities, improve bilingual search which helps both the Germans and global users, and add filters (like full-time or part-time) so that it helps the job seekers to search based on their employment types.
